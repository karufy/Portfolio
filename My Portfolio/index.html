About

Who is Ugo?

Hello, my name is Ugochukwu Ochi, you can call me UGO. A Nigerian by heritage and American by Nationality. I have a Bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering, and I am an IT professional with over 6 years of experience. I have worked for fortune 500 companies such as Walmart, Bank of America, Amazon, Freddie Mac and most recently as a DevOps Engineer at S.W.I.F.T where I deployed and maintained the platform and telecommunication infrastructure for international financial organizations. I am people centric, detail oriented, a fluent communicator, and very analytical. I have led and supervised Agile/ Scrum teams to create E2E; Code to Container deployments using CI/CD and Infrastructure as Code (IaC) templates per customer SLAs. Most importantly, I have created automated systems that reduced toil, saved man hours and increased the bottom-line for the companies I have worked for. Some of my personal projects are on GitHub, HackerRank and Confluence. I cannot detail my professional projects because they are covered by NDAs. I would love to bring my passion for automation, innovation and everything tech to you and ultimately help you achieve your mission goals.

Ugo’s greatest professional strength?

My greatest strength is my problem-solving skills. I pride myself on being able to quickly and efficiently analyze the situation, research for solutions (I sometimes consider different, out-of-the-box perspectives) and effect resolution. This skill allows me to remain productive even in stressful situations.

Ugo’s biggest weakness?

My biggest weakness is public speaking. I get the jitters when I am put in front of an audience. I was once given an award as the best new contract employee in my department. When giving my thank you speech, I was so nervous I was staring at my feet the whole time. Since then I have made strides to remedy this situation by taking online courses in public speaking, and by becoming a mentor in a program called Streetwise Partners for individuals attempting to get into the IT industry. Up until the COVID pandemic precluded it, I had also began teaching an in-person Linux beginner class to students who wanted to break into the industry. I volunteer at Donor Worx Inc, a charity organization for children born with cleft lips, where I walk the streets asking for charity from passersby. I am also a knight of St Columbus and a patron of St Vincent de Paul Charity Organization, where I often speak to congregants about the society.
